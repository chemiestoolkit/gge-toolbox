🔎 Sale value search — money → anything

Pick exactly what you're chasing — attack vs defence troops, siege tools, time skips, Sceat, construction / upgrade tokens, gacha & wheel currencies, offerings — and it ranks every real-money sale by how much of that you get per dollar. Retired seasons are hidden by default so you mostly see current-meta bundles.

⚖️ Value weights — tune what matters (drives the 🏆 Overall ranking. Starts on sensible default tiers — wheel tickets near-zero, event-gacha & affluence high, gear/rubies above neutral — troops & tools already weighted by combat power)
Value model: Attack / Defence troops and siege tools are scored by combat power (qty × atk/def) — a pile of weak units no longer beats a smaller strong pack, and attack vs defence are split so an attack account isn't told a wall-of-defence bundle is great. Time skips are pooled into total hours; everything else scores by quantity. Banners, event-point items and token chests are pulled out of the troop count into their own 🚩/🗡️/👑 buckets so they don't inflate troop value. Gacha & wheels are split out properly: basic Wheel tickets (low value), Affluence tickets (Wheel of Affluence — strong in bulk), Lucky pennies and a catch-all Event gacha currency facet that auto-buckets rotating event currencies (Easter eggs, etc.) when they're live in the captured data — each priced on its own so junk tickets no longer ride along with the good stuff. The ⚖️ weights let you say what you care about (crank Resources for mead, zero-out defence) — that drives the 🏆 Overall ranking; there's no in-game number for cross-category worth, so it's your call, not a guess. On the 🏆 Overall tab each card shows “% vs best deal” — 100% is the strongest-value offer currently shown, everything else is rated against it (so the number always matches the ranking). Same bundle, many sizes: when an offer is sold at several sizes (40 / 80 / 150 troops of the identical pack) they're collapsed into one best-value card with an “also sold in N other sizes” note, so the list isn't flooded with near-duplicates. Relevance: “Hide retired seasons” drops the legacy catalogue (pre-modern offer engine) so you mostly see current bundles — facets that only exist in retired seasons (e.g. hero equipment) are dimmed and tell you to untick it; “Newest era” sort orders by internal offer ID. Prices: exact where the tier matches a known AU pack ($8.99 / $17.99 / $34.99 …), estimated (≈) for the cheaper tiers. The static data holds offer definitions, not your live daily rotation, so search by what you actually see in-game.
