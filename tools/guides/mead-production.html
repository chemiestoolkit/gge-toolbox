🍺 Increasing Mead Production

Guide Economy Everwinter Glacier ~4 min read

Mead is the Everwinter Glacier's version of food. Every soldier and tool you keep in a Glacier castle drinks mead each hour instead of eating food, and your net mead (production − consumption) decides whether you can hold troops up there or watch them desert. The mechanics mirror food almost exactly — same logic, just a different resource and a different kingdom.

How mead actually works

  • It's a Glacier-only resource. Mead is produced and consumed inside the Everwinter Glacier. Your Great Empire farms and food bonuses don't touch it, and mead can't feed troops sitting in your green-kingdom castles.
  • Taverns are your base production. Taverns are to mead what farmhouses are to food — build the full row and keep them maxed for your Glacier castle / outpost.
  • Production is base × (1 + bonuses). Taverns set the base mead/hour; decorations, build items and a few buildings multiply it.
  • Consumption scales with what's parked there. Troops and tools stationed in the Glacier each have a mead upkeep. A heavy defensive stack up there can out-drink a maxed tavern row, so the fastest fix is often moving or thinning the garrison, not building more taverns.
  • Negative net mead isn't instant death. You burn stored mead first; only when the store hits zero do units start leaving. That buffer is a resource you can spend on purpose during Glacier events.

The quick version — push production up

  • Taverns: your foundation. Full row, max level for the castle. This is the single biggest lever — everything else is a multiplier on top of it.
  • Decorations: mead-production decos stack toward a cap, the same way food decos do. Fill toward that cap with your biggest mead-bonus decos before chasing smaller sources. (Exact cap % — verify in-game against your current version.)
  • Build items: permanent mead-production items from offers/events, plus temporary gatsha boosts (big % mead for a few days). Slot the temporary ones right before heavy consumption — a defence wave or a recruit dump in the Glacier.
  • Storage: upgrading your mead storage doesn't make mead, but a bigger buffer is exactly what lets you run net-negative through an event without losing troops.

The other half — cut consumption

  • Don't over-garrison the Glacier. Keep only the troops you actually need up there; park the rest in a green-kingdom castle where they eat food (which is far easier to mass-produce) instead of mead.
  • Tools drink too. Stacks of unused siege tools sitting in a Glacier castle still pull mead upkeep — store or use them.
  • Use the buffer for events. Before a big Glacier defence or recruit push, let mead go net-negative on purpose, ride the stored buffer down, then top taverns / slot a temp mead item to recover.

Rule of thumb: keep taverns maxed, fill the mead deco cap, and treat your mead store as something you spend during events — and remember the cheapest mead is the troop you didn't garrison in the Glacier in the first place.
