The Rift Tournament is a competitive season wrapped around the Rift Raid. While your alliance fights the boss as normal, you now also climb a ladder of six divisions, bank a brand-new currency (Rift Points), clear quests for score, and earn two extra reward tracks on top of the usual boss loot. Do well, rank up, and the prizes get better each season.

It sits on top of the boss fight — it doesn't replace it. You still breach walls and clear the courtyard. The Tournament layers quests, a division leaderboard and the new Rift currencies over the top. New to the rift? Read Rift Raid Basics first.

The six divisions

Every alliance sits in one of six divisions. The higher your division, the more Tournament levels you can reach and the better the reward pool. At the end of each window your alliance is ranked against the others in its sub-division and moves up or down for next time.

Copper → Glass → Bronze → Silver → Gold → Diamond — Diamond (added in this rework) is the new top tier. From season two, promotion/demotion runs by placement in your sub-division, with the exact counts now confirmed from the in-game rules:

This first run (Distribution phase) works differently: everyone is in Copper on one big division leaderboard, and final placement seeds next season — top 36 alliances → Gold, 37–96 → Silver, 97–456 → Bronze, 457–1,356 → Glass, the rest stay Copper. Diamond isn't seeded directly; it's reached by winning a Gold sub-division from season two.

Rift Points — the new currency

The Tournament introduces Rift Points, which replace the old Activity Points. You earn them in battle (breaching walls, clearing courtyards, killing units), and there's now gear that boosts the rate — "+X% Rift Points earned in battle." Rift Points do two jobs: they gate the Boss Defeat Rewards (hit the point threshold on a boss to claim), and they feed your quests.

How you score: the Quest Board

Score comes from quests, not the boss directly. Quests come in three difficulties — bronze, silver and gold — and each one you complete adds points that push your alliance up the Tournament level track.

RuleDetail (in-game rules, relaunch)
Quest boardup to 25 quests on the board at once; you can only have one accepted at a time
Quest ticketsstart with 2; earn +2 every Rift Tournament level your alliance gains; buy up to 3 more at 7,500 rubies each (bought tickets don't carry over)
Acceptingone ticket per quest; finish within the time limit or the quest fails and the ticket is gone. Completed-but-unclaimed quests auto-claim when the event ends
Rerollingfree — any participating member can reroll a quest that nobody has accepted yet. Claiming, failing or rerolling puts that slot on cooldown (skippable with rubies)

GGS's own texts disagree on the ticket-purchase cap (the rules say 5, the buy popup says 1) — the shop config itself says 3 per event, so that's what we list.

Quest objectives cover the whole toolkit of rift play:

  • Slay a number of the boss's specific units
  • Consume battle/support tools in an attack
  • Gather Rift Points on walls or in the courtyard
  • Finish courtyard attacks within a time limit
  • Open Rift chests and collect event currencies

The two reward tracks

On top of the usual Boss Defeat loot, the Tournament pays out two ways:

TrackHow you get it
Level rewardsEvery time your alliance crosses a point threshold and gains a Tournament level, every eligible member is paid out automatically. This replaces the old Activity Rewards. Higher division = more levels = better payouts.
Ranking rewardsGiven to the top alliances in your sub-division at the end of the window, scaled by division. Sub-division winners also take the Rift Tournament Trophy.
Eligibility — the 250-point floor. To receive any Tournament rewards you must contribute at least 250 Rift Tournament Points to your alliance (check the contributions tab). And if you leave your alliance during the event, you forfeit your ranking rewards — move castles after the window, not during it.

Quick takeaways

  • Hit 250 points early — under the eligibility floor you get nothing, however well the alliance does. Then keep quests turning over.
  • Level ups snowball — every Tournament level pays out and hands everyone +2 quest tickets for more score attempts.
  • Reroll is free — any member can reroll an unaccepted quest, so curate the board for your hitters. But don't let an accepted quest fail: the ticket is gone for good.
  • Don't leave your alliance mid-event — you forfeit the ranking rewards you've earned.
  • This run decides your seed — top 36 alliances jump straight to Gold next season. A hard push now skips months of climbing.
