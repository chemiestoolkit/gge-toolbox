🏆 Rift Tournament

Event Rift Raid Alliance Divisions ~5 min read
⚠️ Work in progress — not yet complete or verified. The Rift Tournament went live very recently (game version 776.06). The mechanics below are pulled straight from the game's own data files, so they're accurate — but the exact reward amounts (how much of each item you get per division, level and placement) are decided server-side and aren't in the client, so those are marked "see in-game" until they're confirmed from live reward screens. Treat numbers as structure, not gospel.

The Rift Tournament is the new alliance meta-layer wrapped around the Rift Raid. While you fight the boss as usual, your alliance now also climbs a ladder of six divisions, completes quests for points, and earns two whole new reward tracks on top of the normal boss loot. Think of it as a season: do well, rank up, win better prizes next time.

It sits on top of the boss fight — it doesn't replace it. You still breach walls and clear the courtyard for Boss Defeat Rewards. The Tournament just adds quests, a division leaderboard, and the new Rift currencies. If the rift itself is new to you, read Rift Raid Basics first.

The six divisions

Every alliance sits in one of six divisions. The higher your division, the more Rift Tournament Levels you can reach — and the better the rewards get. At the end of each event your alliance is ranked against the others in your sub-group and moves up or down for next time.

Copper → Glass → Bronze → Silver → Gold → Diamond (Diamond is the top). In the standard "Ranking" mode, promotions and demotions work by your placement in your sub-division:

First time your alliance joins, you start in Copper. A one-off "seeding" event slots everyone into a starting division before normal ranking begins.

How you score: the Quest Board

Points come from quests, not from the boss directly. The Quest Board offers up to 25 quests at once, tied to the boss level your alliance is on. Every quest you claim adds Rift Tournament Points to the alliance total — and crossing point thresholds raises your Rift Tournament Level.

RuleDetail

Acceptone at a time; each accept costs 1 Rift Quest Ticket

Time limiteach quest runs 6 hours; fail it and the ticket is gone
Rerollany un-accepted quest can be rerolled; reroll/claim/fail puts the slot on cooldown (skip with rubies)

There are 12 quest types — a mix of building troops, using tools, killing the boss's units, and earning Rift Points:

  • Courtyard Raider / Wall Breacher — earn Rift Points on the courtyard / walls of a boss.
  • Courtyard / Wall Hunter & Eliminator — kill a number of specific (or any) units there.
  • Melee / Ranged Supremacy & Bastion — obtain units above an attack / defence threshold.
  • Tool Deployment — consume support tools attacking a boss.
  • Treasure Hunter — open loot boxes.
  • Rift Shopper / Collector / Spender — buy, obtain, or spend event currency.

The two reward tracks

On top of the usual Boss Defeat loot, the Tournament pays out two ways. To be eligible for anything, you must contribute at least 250 Rift Tournament Points.

TrackHow you get it
Level RewardsEach time your alliance crosses a point threshold and gains a Rift Tournament Level, every eligible member gets a reward. Higher division = more levels = better rewards.
Ranking RewardsGiven to the top alliances in your sub-division at the end of the event. The #1 alliance also earns the Rift Tournament Trophy.

What you can win

The new reward pool (all confirmed present in the game data) is built around division prestige and a fresh "Riftsliver" theme:

  • Division Castellan & Commander skins — Bronze / Silver / Gold / Diamond. A map reskin of your castles (Castellan) and your marching army (Commander). Only the top four divisions get these.
  • Riftsliver decorations — 16 in all: Relic, Wellspring, Nexus and Bulwark, each in Bronze / Silver / Gold / Diamond. Browse them in the Decorations overview →
  • Riftsliver gear — a Castellan-slot Amulet and Loop per tier.
  • "Rift Raid Conqueror" alliance crest layout — per division.
  • Premium Rift Boxes (Bronze / Silver / Gold) and the Rift Tournament event reward set — see the Loot Box overview →
  • Currencies: Rift Points (earned in combat — they gate Boss Defeat Rewards and feed quests) and Rift Quest Tickets.
The exact amounts aren't public yet. Which division / level / placement gives how many of each item is delivered by the game server at event time and isn't in the client files — so this guide can't pin those numbers down. The fastest way to lock them in: open the in-game reward hub and the division reward panels during a live event and screenshot them.

Quick takeaways

  • Hit 250 points personally or you get nothing — spend your tickets.
  • Quests give points; points raise your alliance level; levels (and your division) decide rewards.
  • Climbing divisions is the long game — a higher division unlocks more levels and better prizes each season.
  • Don't waste Quest Tickets on quests you can't finish in 6 hours; reroll the bad ones early.
